**Playoff Dust Settles: Flat-Earthers Survive as Semi-Final Brackets Ignite**
A razor-thin goal differential catapults the Flat-Earthers into a surprise deep run.

## Round 1 Autopsy: Series Decided

The opening round of the DMHL Low B playoffs concluded with a trio of decisive victories. Top seed **Don Cherry's** exhibited formidable dominance, securing their series against the **Muffin Men** with a commanding 6-0 victory in Game 2, exploiting a pathetic lack of effort from a Muffin Men squad that looked completely lazy on the backcheck. Michael Murphy led the charge, netting two goals and adding an assist, while Sean Murphy also contributed two goals. Ryan Willms and Colin Digiacomo each tallied two assists in the clean sheet performance, played before a notably larger crowd at St. Mikes Arena. Don Cherry's completed the sweep with 4 points and an overwhelming +10 goal differential.

**The Sahara** advanced in a tighter affair, defeating **4 Lines** with a series score of 3 points to 1. Their Game 2 concluded in a 1-1 tie, with Adam Miller scoring for 4 Lines (assisted by Luke Gravett) and Kosta Likourezos finding the net for The Sahara (assisted by Isaac Jeffers) late in the third period. This single point was enough for The Sahara to clinch the series. Drew Ingall of The Sahara received the only penalty of the match, a minor for tripping.

In another closely contested series, **The Shockers** prevailed over the **Flat-Earthers**, also accumulating 3 points to their opponent's 1. Game 2 saw The Shockers emerge with a 2-1 victory. Despite a power-play goal from Reid Chessell (assisted by Marcus Simmonds and Matthew McIntosh) for the Flat-Earthers in the second period, The Shockers rallied in the third. Keelan Hughes tied the game, assisted by Levi Tobe, before Patrick Mcmullan netted the game-winner, assisted by Josh Pope. The Flat-Earthers' short bench of nine players, as noted by observers, presented a considerable challenge.

## The Lucky Loser Miracle

While the Muffin Men (0 points, -10 Goal Diff) and 4 Lines (1 point, -2 Goal Diff) saw their playoff aspirations end, the **Flat-Earthers** received a reprieve. Despite their series loss, their single point and a superior goal differential of -1 edged out 4 Lines, granting them the coveted Lucky Loser berth. This narrow margin ensures their improbable continuation in the tournament.

## Semi-Final Bracket Set

The Semi-Finals are now officially set. Top-seeded **Don Cherry's** (4 points, +10 Goal Diff) will face the **Flat-Earthers** (1 point, -1 Goal Diff) in a compelling matchup of a powerhouse against a team with a new lease on life. The other bracket sees **The Sahara** (3 points, +2 Goal Diff) take on **The Shockers** (3 points, +1 Goal Diff).

### Semi-Final Scouting Report: Tale of the Tape

* **Don Cherry's vs. Flat-Earthers:** During the regular season, Don Cherry's finished first with 30 points and a league-leading 87 goals for, while the Flat-Earthers were second with 23 points and 76 goals for. The Flat-Earthers, with 79 regular season penalty minutes compared to Don Cherry's 57, demonstrated a penchant for more physical play, which could translate into a high-PIM series.

* **The Sahara vs. The Shockers:** The regular season standings hint at a tightly contested series. The Sahara (17 points, 62 goals for) and The Shockers (16 points, 62 goals for) were nearly identical in offensive output and overall standing. However, The Shockers accumulated a notable 88 penalty minutes in the regular season, significantly more than The Sahara's 54, suggesting their battles could be penalty-laden affairs.
