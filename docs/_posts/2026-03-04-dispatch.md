### Playoff Picture Clears: Shockers and Sahara Advance, Flat-Earthers Snatch Lucky Loser Spot

The DMHL Monday/Wednesday Low B playoffs have completed Round 1, leaving a familiar top seed facing an improbable survivor, and a compelling battle between two gritty contenders.

## The Round 1 Autopsy

Don Cherry's continued their regular season dominance by sweeping the Muffin Men in their series, securing a formidable 6-0 victory in the decisive game. Michael Murphy led the charge with a pair of goals (05:59 1st Period, 01:02 2nd Period), complemented by Cassady Louks' early tally (04:39 1st Period) and a strong two-goal performance from Sean Murphy (03:08 1st Period, 13:19 3rd Period). Dongyeon Kim also added a goal (04:59 2nd Period) to complete the rout, which saw Don Cherry's outscore their opponents 11-1 over two games. Referees Russell Nichols and Evan Benwell presided over a remarkably clean affair, calling zero penalties.

In a tighter battle, The Sahara advanced past 4 Lines in a series that culminated in a 1-1 tie. 4 Lines' Adam Miller opened the scoring at 10:48 of the third period (assisted by Luke Gravett), but Kosta Likourezos delivered a crucial equalizer for The Sahara at 01:03 of the third period (assisted by Isaac Jeffers), securing the series win with three points to 4 Lines' one. Drew Ingall received the game's sole penalty, a minor for tripping.

The Shockers also punched their ticket with a 2-1 victory over the Flat-Earthers, sealing their series with three points. After Reid Chessell opened the scoring on a power play at 00:38 of the second period for the Flat-Earthers (assisted by Marcus Simmonds and Matthew McIntosh), The Shockers mounted a comeback. Keelan Hughes tied the game at 10:19 of the third period (assisted by Levi Tobe), followed by Patrick Mcmullan's series-winning goal at 05:46 of the third period (assisted by Josh Pope). The Flat-Earthers played with a short bench, a factor that may have influenced the late game dynamic.

## The Semi-Final Bracket

With Round 1 complete, the Semi-Finals are set:

*   **Don Cherry's (Rk 1) vs. Flat-Earthers (Lucky Loser)**
*   **The Sahara (Rk 2) vs. The Shockers (Rk 3)**

## The Lucky Loser Miracle

The Flat-Earthers, despite their 2-1 loss to The Shockers, earned a reprieve as the lucky loser. Of the three teams eliminated, the Flat-Earthers secured one point and a goal differential of -1, a stark contrast to 4 Lines' -2 differential and the Muffin Men's -10. This narrow margin demonstrates the unforgiving nature of playoff tiebreakers and the Flat-Earthers' relative resilience.

## Semi-Final Scouting Reports

**Don Cherry's vs. Flat-Earthers:** This matchup pits the top regular season squad, Don Cherry's, against the lucky loser. Don Cherry's was an offensive juggernaut in the regular season, leading the league with 87 goals for and a remarkable +33 goal differential. Michael Murphy (20G, 16A) and Sean Murphy (11G, 20A) consistently powered their attack. The Flat-Earthers, ranked second in the regular season, were a more defensively responsible team, with a +1 goal differential. Their leading scorer, Anthony Pennino (14G, 10A), will need to find another gear to challenge Don Cherry's formidable defense. Past regular season encounters suggest Don Cherry's will aim for a high-scoring affair, while the Flat-Earthers will try to slow the pace.

**The Sahara vs. The Shockers:** This looks to be a grinder. The Sahara finished the regular season with a -22 goal differential but have found playoff success through tight, low-scoring games. The Shockers finished with a neutral goal differential in the regular season, often engaging in physical contests, as evidenced by their league-high 88 penalty minutes. This series is unlikely to be a high-scoring affair; expect a battle for every inch of ice.
