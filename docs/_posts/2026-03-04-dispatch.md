**Post-Mortem and Pathway: Playoff Field Narrows as Lucky Loser Emerges**
Round 1 concludes, setting a dramatic Semi-Final bracket featuring a pivotal goal differential tiebreaker.

The opening round of the DMHL Monday/Wednesday Low B playoffs delivered its share of definitive performances and nail-biting finishes. Don Cherry's asserted their regular season dominance in a commanding 6-0 victory over the Muffin Men at St. Mikes Arena. Michael Murphy, with two goals and an assist, alongside Sean Murphy's two goals, spearheaded an offensive onslaught that saw them take the series 4-0. The Muffin Men were unable to generate sustained pressure against the top seed.

In a tightly contested affair, The Sahara secured their semi-final berth with a 1-1 tie against 4 Lines, clinching their series 3-1. Kosta Likourezos opened the scoring for The Sahara in the third period, only for Adam Miller (assisted by Luke Gravett) to equalize for 4 Lines late in the game, forcing the draw.

The Shockers completed their series victory, prevailing 2-1 over the Flat-Earthers. Reid Chessell provided the Flat-Earthers' lone power-play goal (assisted by Marcus Simmonds and Matthew McIntosh) in the second period, but The Shockers rallied in the third. Keelan Hughes, assisted by Levi Tobe, tied the contest before Patrick Mcmullan, with the help of Josh Pope, netted the decisive goal. The Flat-Earthers, short-benched with only nine skaters, showed resilience but couldn't withstand the late surge.

The Semi-Finals are now set. Don Cherry's, The Sahara, and The Shockers advance as series winners. The fourth spot, the coveted 'lucky loser' position, goes to the Flat-Earthers. Their series goal differential of -1 narrowly surpassed 4 Lines' -2, earning them a reprieve.

This sets up two compelling Semi-Final matchups:

**Don Cherry's vs. Flat-Earthers:**
The top-seeded Don Cherry's, who finished the regular season with a league-best 30 points and a staggering +33 goal differential, face the Flat-Earthers, ranked second in the regular season but with a tighter +1 differential. Don Cherry's offensive firepower, led by Michael Murphy (36 points) and Sean Murphy (31 points), will test the Flat-Earthers' resilience, who will rely on Anthony Pennino (24 points) and Reid Chessell (22 points) to generate offense.

**The Sahara vs. The Shockers:**
This promises a competitive series between two teams that finished fourth and fifth, respectively, in the regular season. The Sahara, with a -22 regular season goal differential, and The Shockers, at an even 0, suggest a more defensive battle. The Shockers' Caden Bower (21 points) and Patrick Mcmullan (18 points) will contend with The Sahara's Wes Martin (17 points) and Kosta Likourezos (16 points) in what could be a grind.

**Three Stars**
1.  **Michael Murphy (Don Cherry's):** 2 Goals, 1 Assist in a dominant 6-0 series-clinching victory.
2.  **Sean Murphy (Don Cherry's):** 2 Goals, contributing significantly to his team's comprehensive win.
3.  **Patrick Mcmullan (The Shockers):** Netted the game-winning goal in the third period of a tight 2-1 playoff contest.
