**Playoff Dust Settles: Flat-Earthers Forge a Path as Semis Loom**
The DMHL Low B playoffs roared through Round 1, delivering dramatic finishes and confirming the semi-final matchups, with one lucky loser defying the odds.

### Round 1 Autopsy

The top-seeded Don Cherry's asserted their dominance over the Muffin Men, sweeping the series 4-0 in points. Their 6-0 shutout victory in Game 2 at St. Mikes Arena, witnessed by a larger-than-normal crowd, was a clinic in offensive execution. Michael Murphy led the charge with two goals and an assist, while Sean Murphy also buried two. Cassady Louks added a goal and an assist, with Dongyeon Kim also finding the back of the net. Ryan Willms and Colin Digiacomo each contributed two assists.

In a grind of a series, The Sahara clinched their spot by besting 4 Lines with three points to one. Their decisive moment arrived in Game 2, a hard-fought 1-1 tie. 4 Lines' Adam Miller opened the scoring, assisted by Luke Gravett, before Kosta Likourezos, with an assist from Isaac Jeffers, notched the equalizer for The Sahara late in the third period. The only penalty of the contest went to Drew Ingall of The Sahara for tripping.

The Shockers secured their semi-final berth in a tight 2-1 Game 2 battle against the Flat-Earthers, winning the series 3-1 in points. Despite playing with a short bench, the Flat-Earthers struck first on the power play with a goal from Reid Chessell, assisted by Marcus Simmonds and Matthew McIntosh. The Shockers responded in the third, as Keelan Hughes, assisted by Levi Tobe, tied the game, and Patrick Mcmullan then netted the series-winning goal, assisted by Josh Pope. Referees Russell Nichols and Evan Benwell officiated all three intense matchups this week.

### The Semi-Final Bracket

The dust has cleared, and the Semi-Finals are set:

*   **Don Cherry's vs. Flat-Earthers**
*   **The Sahara vs. The Shockers**

### The Lucky Loser Miracle

The Flat-Earthers emerge as the 'Lucky Loser' to complete the semi-final bracket. Despite earning only one point in their series, their superior goal differential of -1 edged out 4 Lines (-2) and the Muffin Men (-10), securing their improbable return to contention.

### Scouting Report: Tale of the Tape

**Don Cherry's vs. Flat-Earthers**
This matchup pits the regular season's top two teams against each other. Don Cherry's finished first with 30 points and an impressive +33 goal differential, while the Flat-Earthers were second with 23 points and a +1 differential. Don Cherry's boasts an offensive arsenal, including Michael Murphy (36 points) and Sean Murphy (31 points). The Flat-Earthers, anchored by Anthony Pennino (24 points) and Reid Chessell (22 points), will need to tighten their defensive play and manage their penalty minutes, having accrued 79 in the regular season compared to Don Cherry's 57.

**The Sahara vs. The Shockers**
This pairing brings together two teams that finished close in the regular season standings, with The Sahara (17 points, -22 goal differential) narrowly ahead of The Shockers (16 points, 0 goal differential). The Shockers are known for their physical play, accumulating 88 penalty minutes in the regular season, significantly more than The Sahara's 54. Key players like Caden Bower (21 points) and Patrick Mcmullan (18 points) will drive The Shockers' attack, while The Sahara will rely on Wes Martin (17 points) and Kosta Likourezos (16 points).
