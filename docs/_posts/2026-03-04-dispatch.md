**Playoff Dust Settles: Flat-Earthers Earn Reprieve in DMHL Semi-Final Bracket Lock**
The Low B playoffs have wrapped Round 1, delivering definitive victors and an unexpected lucky loser for the semi-final matchups.

**Round 1 Autopsy: High Stakes and Clutch Finishes**
Don Cherry's emphatically swept the Muffin Men, culminating in a dominant 6-0 victory. Michael Murphy spearheaded the offense with two goals and an assist, joined by Sean Murphy's two goals, a marker from Cassady Louks (with an assist from Michael Murphy), and Dongyeon Kim's contribution. The Muffin Men were eliminated, concluding their playoff run with zero points.

The Sahara advanced in a grinder against 4 Lines, securing their spot with a 1-1 tie. Kosta Likourezos gave The Sahara a late third-period lead, but Adam Miller quickly responded for 4 Lines to equalize, sealing The Sahara's passage with 3 points.

The Shockers completed a dramatic comeback against the Flat-Earthers in a 2-1 decision. Reid Chessell put the Flat-Earthers on the board with a power-play goal. However, The Shockers rallied in the third period, with Keelan Hughes tying the game before Patrick Mcmullan netted the series-winning goal.

**The Lucky Loser Miracle: Flat-Earthers' Second Chance**
Among the eliminated, the Flat-Earthers found a lifeline as the "Lucky Loser." Despite finishing Round 1 with 1 point, their -1 goal differential narrowly bested 4 Lines' -2, earning them a berth in the Semi-Finals. The Muffin Men, with a -10 goal differential, were out of contention for this reprieve.

**Semi-Final Bracket Set**
The stage is now set for two compelling Semi-Final series:
*   **Don Cherry's vs. Flat-Earthers**
*   **The Sahara vs. The Shockers**

**Tale of the Tape: Semi-Final Scouting Report**
**Don Cherry's vs. Flat-Earthers:** This matchup pits the regular season juggernaut, Don Cherry's (1st, 30 Pts, +33 goal differential), against the Flat-Earthers (2nd, 23 Pts, +1 goal differential). Don Cherry's formidable attack, led by Michael Murphy (36 Pts) and Sean Murphy (31 Pts), will challenge the Flat-Earthers' resilience. The Flat-Earthers (79 regular season PIMs) have historically taken more penalties than Don Cherry's (57 PIMs).

**The Sahara vs. The Shockers:** A tightly contested series is expected between The Sahara (4th, 17 Pts, -22 goal differential) and The Shockers (5th, 16 Pts, 0 goal differential). The Shockers demonstrated a tighter defensive structure in the regular season (62 goals against) compared to The Sahara (84 goals against). The Shockers' Caden Bower (21 Pts) and Patrick Mcmullan (18 Pts) will be key against The Sahara's Wes Martin (17 Pts) and Kosta Likourezos (16 Pts). Officials Evan Benwell and Russell Nichols oversaw the Round 1 action with minimal penalties called, suggesting a focus on free-flowing play.
