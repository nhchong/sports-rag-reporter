The Postseason Shake-Up: Flat-Earthers Emerge as Lucky Loser, Semis Set for High Stakes

Round 1 of the DMHL Monday/Wednesday Low B playoffs delivered decisive blows and a miraculous reprieve. The dust has settled, and the Semi-Final bracket is now officially drawn.

### Round 1 Autopsy

Don Cherry's wasted no time asserting their top seed dominance over the Muffin Men. After a commanding Game 1, they completed the sweep with a resounding 6-0 victory. Michael Murphy led the charge, scoring twice (with assists from Ryan Willms and Cassady Louks on his first, and Colin Digiacomo and Sam Pasich on his second). Cassady Louks added a goal (assisted by Jack Pirie and Michael Murphy), Sean Murphy netted two (assisted by Koch Tsatsos and Colin Digiacomo), and Dongyeon Kim rounded out the scoring (assisted by Ryan Willms). Don Cherry's secured their series 4-0 in points, displaying a clinical offensive attack that even the convenor, Dan Hickox, noted as "controlled play from start to finish."

In a battle of strategic attrition, The Sahara clinched their series against 4 Lines. Game 2 concluded in a tight 1-1 tie, a testament to both teams' defensive focus. 4 Lines initially took the lead with Adam Miller’s goal (assisted by Luke Gravett) in the third period, only for Kosta Likourezos to equalize for The Sahara (assisted by Isaac Jeffers) later in the period. This draw, following a hard-fought Game 1, saw The Sahara advance with a 3-1 series points advantage.

The Shockers secured their Semi-Final berth by defeating the Flat-Earthers 2-1 in a nail-biting Game 2. The Flat-Earthers’ Reid Chessell opened the scoring on the power play (assisted by Marcus Simmonds and Matthew McIntosh) in the second period, but The Shockers responded in the third. Keelan Hughes tied the game (assisted by Levi Tobe), and Patrick Mcmullan then netted the series-clinching goal (assisted by Josh Pope). The Shockers won the series 3-1 in points.

### The Lucky Loser Miracle

Among the defeated, only one team could return, and that miracle belongs to the **Flat-Earthers**. While both the Flat-Earthers and 4 Lines finished with 1 playoff point, the Flat-Earthers' superior goal differential of -1 edged out 4 Lines' -2, and decisively surpassed the Muffin Men's -10. Their narrow loss, coupled with a better statistical showing, earned them a second life.

### The Semi-Final Bracket

The road to the Final is now clear:

*   **Don Cherry's vs. Flat-Earthers**
*   **The Sahara vs. The Shockers**

### Scouting Report: The Semi-Finals

**Don Cherry's vs. Flat-Earthers**

This matchup pits the regular season's top-ranked Don Cherry's, who boasted a league-leading 87 goals and a +33 differential, against the second-ranked Flat-Earthers, a team that also scored frequently (76 goals) but conceded 75 during the regular campaign. Don Cherry's displayed flawless playoff form with a +10 goal differential, while the Flat-Earthers enter as the lucky loser with a -1 playoff differential. Expect Don Cherry's relentless offensive machine, featuring top scorers like Michael Murphy (36 Pts) and Sean Murphy (31 Pts), to challenge the Flat-Earthers' defensive resolve, which will need to tighten up considerably.

**The Sahara vs. The Shockers**

This promises a tight, grind-it-out affair. The Sahara, ranked fourth in the regular season, managed 62 goals but struggled defensively with 84 goals against. However, they demonstrated a playoff resilience with a +2 goal differential through their two games. The Shockers, while finishing fifth in the regular season, were a more defensively sound squad, allowing only 62 goals. Their 14 PIM in the playoffs also hints at a physical approach. Key players like The Sahara’s Wes Martin (17 Pts) and Kosta Likourezos (16 Pts), and The Shockers’ Caden Bower (21 Pts) and Patrick Mcmullan (18 Pts), will likely dictate the pace in what could be a low-scoring, intense series.
