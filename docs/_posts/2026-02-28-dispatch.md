**Playoff Race Ignites: Top Seeds Punch Early Tickets, Others Scramble for Round 2 Berth**
Don Cherry's and The Sahara take commanding leads, while Flat-Earthers and The Shockers remain deadlocked after the intense playoff opener at St. Mikes Arena.

The postseason drama commenced Wednesday evening at St. Mikes Arena, setting a vibrant tone for the Monday/Wednesday Low B division playoffs.

**Don Cherry's Assert Dominance Over Muffin Men**
In the 1-seed vs 6-seed matchup, Don Cherry's wasted no time in establishing their playoff intentions with a resounding 5-1 victory over the Muffin Men. Don Cherry's displayed significant offensive control, tallying two power play goals from Michael Murphy and Sean Murphy, and an even strength marker from Dongyeon Kim in the second period alone, effectively building a lead that Muffin Men couldn't bridge. Dongyeon Kim led the offensive charge for Don Cherry's with a goal and two assists. Muffin Men's Cosimo Morin netted their sole goal in the first period and added an assist. Don Cherry's now sit comfortably at 2 points in the 'Race to Three'; a tie or win in Game 2 secures their spot in Round 2. The Muffin Men, with 0 points, face a must-win scenario in Game 2, needing a victory and an OT/shootout win to advance.

**The Sahara Outduels 4 Lines in High-Scoring Affair**
The 2-seed, The Sahara, claimed a 6-4 win against 5-seed 4 Lines in a contest that saw offensive outbursts from both sides. 4 Lines' Adam Miller put up a two-goal performance, including an early first-period marker, keeping his team in contention. Shane Ferguson added a shorthanded tally for 4 Lines in the third period. However, The Sahara’s balanced attack proved too much. Their offensive pressure was persistent, culminating in a goal from Brendan Hancock with just 21 seconds remaining in regulation, sealing the victory. Referee Evan Benwell presided over this affair, which included a "Too Many Men" penalty against 4 Lines. The Sahara now holds 2 points, requiring only a tie or win in Game 2 to move on. 4 Lines, at 0 points, must secure a Game 2 win, followed by an OT/shootout victory.

**Flat-Earthers and The Shockers Lock Horns in Gritty Tie**
The 3-seed Flat-Earthers and 4-seed The Shockers battled to a tense 1-1 tie. Flat-Earthers' Marcus Simmonds opened the scoring in the first, only to be answered by The Shockers' Alex Matheson a minute later. With Flat-Earthers operating with only two substitutes, the game was a physical contest marked by increased penalty minutes from both sides, as officiated by Evan Benwell and Brad Kuchar. Brandon Sanders of the Flat-Earthers was assessed a double minor for Roughing and Elbowing late in the third, while Caden Bower received a double minor for High-Sticking earlier in the period for The Shockers. Both teams now have 1 point in the series. The winner of Game 2 (or OT/shootout, if tied after regulation) will clinch the series.

**Series Math & Lucky Loser Projections**
Don Cherry's and The Sahara are in strong positions, each needing only one point in Game 2 to advance. The Flat-Earthers and The Shockers face a winner-take-all situation in their next matchup. Among the teams currently at 0 points, 4 Lines holds a Goal Differential of -2, positioning them ahead of the Muffin Men (-4) in the preliminary 'Lucky Loser' race. As the stakes escalate, Round 2 will bring immense pressure to bear on teams that will need to elevate their play.

**Three Stars of the Week:**
*   **1st Star: Dongyeon Kim (Don Cherry's)** – A goal and two assists in a dominant playoff opener, leading his team's offensive surge.
*   **2nd Star: Adam Miller (4 Lines)** – Scored two goals in Game 1, providing a significant offensive spark and keeping his team competitive.
*   **3rd Star: Brendan Hancock (The Sahara)** – Netted a clutch goal with 21 seconds left in the third period to seal the victory for his team.
