The DMHL Low B playoffs ignited with fervent intensity, and as the dust settles on Round 1, the semi-final bracket is officially set. While three teams advanced directly, the drama of the 'Lucky Loser' kept fans on edge.

### Round 1 Autopsy

The top-seeded Don Cherry's asserted their dominance over the Muffin Men, sweeping the series with a formidable goal differential. In their initial encounter, the Muffin Men’s Cosimo Morin briefly gave them a lead, but Don Cherry’s quickly took control, with Jack Pirie, Dongyeon Kim, Michael Murphy (on the power play), Sean Murphy (also on the power play), and Sam Pasich all finding the net. The second game saw Don Cherry's dismantle the Muffin Men with a commanding 6-0 shutout. Michael Murphy tallied two goals, mirroring Sean Murphy's two, while Cassady Louks and Dongyeon Kim rounded out the scoring, securing Don Cherry's series victory with 4 points. Referees Russell Nichols and Evan Benwell presided over this game, allowing for a flowing contest with no penalties called.

The series between 4 Lines and The Sahara was a more tightly contested battle for control. The Sahara took the first game in a high-scoring 6-4 affair, with Kosta Likourezos netting two pivotal goals. 4 Lines kept pace through Adam Miller, Thomas Bevilacqua, and a shorthanded effort by Shane Ferguson. The deciding game culminated in a tense 1-1 tie. Adam Miller put 4 Lines ahead in the third period, only for Kosta Likourezos to deliver a critical equalizer for The Sahara with just 1:03 remaining, securing the tie and the 3 points needed to advance. This low-penalty game, overseen by Russell Nichols and Evan Benwell, saw only one minor infraction assessed.

In the Flat-Earthers' clash against The Shockers, the series was defined by resilience. Their opening game ended in a hard-fought 1-1 draw, with Marcus Simmonds scoring for the Flat-Earthers and Alex Matheson responding for The Shockers. This matchup, refereed by Evan Benwell, Brad Kuchar, and Russell Nichols, saw an elevated penalty count with eight minors and a double minor each for Caden Bower and Brandon Sanders. The Shockers ultimately clinched the series with a narrow 2-1 victory in the second game. Flat-Earthers’ Reid Chessell opened the scoring on the power play, but The Shockers rallied with goals from Keelan Hughes and a clutch game-winner from Patrick Mcmullan at 5:46 in the third period, securing their 3 series points.

### The Lucky Loser Miracle

The Flat-Earthers, despite losing their series, clinched the coveted Lucky Loser spot. Their series goal differential of -1 was superior to 4 Lines' -2 and the Muffin Men's -10, proving that every goal matters, even in defeat.

### Semi-Final Bracket Set

The path to the final is now clear, with two compelling matchups awaiting:

*   **Don Cherry's (1st Seed) vs. Flat-Earthers (Lucky Loser)**
*   **The Sahara (2nd Seed) vs. The Shockers (3rd Seed)**

### Scouting Report: Tale of the Tape

**Don Cherry's vs. Flat-Earthers:**
The regular season history between these two paints a clear picture: Don Cherry's holds a significant edge with a 3-0-1 record. They dominated the goal differential, securing victories with scores such as 9-2 and 4-2, alongside a 5-5 tie. These past meetings were consistently high-scoring affairs but generally low in penalty minutes, suggesting an emphasis on offensive execution over physical play.

**The Sahara vs. The Shockers:**
The Shockers hold the historical advantage over The Sahara in the regular season, boasting a 2-1-0 record. They delivered decisive victories with scores of 7-1 and 5-0, demonstrating their offensive capability. The Sahara managed only one win, a narrow 3-2 victory. Similar to the other matchup, these games were not defined by excessive penalty minutes, indicating a focus on disciplined play.
