### Round One Rout: Favorites Advance as Flat-Earthers Snatch Lucky Loser Spot
Dominant Performances Seal Series Wins, While Goal Differential Becomes a Playoff Lifeboat

The opening round of the Monday/Wednesday Low B playoffs delivered a mix of commanding victories and nail-biting finishes. Three teams swiftly punched their tickets to Round 2, while the ‘Lucky Loser’ race boiled down to a single goal. All playoff contests this week unfolded at St. Mikes Arena, setting the stage for high-stakes action.

Don Cherry's demonstrated why they finished atop the regular season standings, dispatching the Muffin Men in two games. Game 1 on Wednesday saw Don Cherry's overcome an early Cosimo Morin goal (12:04, 1st period) to secure a 5-1 victory. Michael Murphy, Sean Murphy, and Dongyeon Kim each tallied a goal and an assist in the decisive opener. On Monday, Don Cherry's completed the sweep with a dominant 6-0 shutout. Michael Murphy led the charge with two goals and an assist, while Sean Murphy added two goals, extending their team's perfect series record to 4 points.

The Sahara outlasted 4 Lines to clinch their series with 3 points. In Wednesday's Game 1, The Sahara secured a 6-4 victory over 4 Lines, fueled by multiple-point efforts from Derrick Wong, Kosta Likourezos, and Wil Nenadovic. 4 Lines forward Adam Miller netted two goals in a valiant effort. Monday's Game 2 proved to be a tighter affair, ending in a 1-1 stalemate. Adam Miller put 4 Lines on the board at 10:48 of the third period, but Kosta Likourezos responded for The Sahara at 1:03 of the same frame to force the tie and clinch the series.

In a tightly contested matchup, The Shockers edged out the Flat-Earthers, advancing with 3 series points. Game 1 on Wednesday ended in a 1-1 draw, with Marcus Simmonds scoring for the Flat-Earthers and Alex Matheson finding the net for The Shockers. Monday's Game 2 saw The Shockers claim a narrow 2-1 victory. Reid Chessell, capitalizing on a power play at 0:38 in the second period, briefly gave the Flat-Earthers the lead. However, Keelan Hughes tied it at 10:19 of the third, and Patrick Mcmullan scored the series-clinching goal for The Shockers at 5:46 in the final frame.

**Series Math**
*   **Don Cherry's (4 Pts)** vs Muffin Men (0 Pts) - **Don Cherry's wins series.**
*   4 Lines (1 Pts) vs **The Sahara (3 Pts)** - **The Sahara wins series.**
*   Flat-Earthers (1 Pts) vs **The Shockers (3 Pts)** - **The Shockers wins series.**

**The Lucky Loser**
With Don Cherry's, The Sahara, and The Shockers advancing, attention turns to the three eliminated teams for the sole Lucky Loser spot.
*   **Flat-Earthers:** 1 point, -1 Goal Differential
*   **4 Lines:** 1 point, -2 Goal Differential
*   **Muffin Men:** 0 points, -10 Goal Differential

Based on series points and then goal differential, **Flat-Earthers** claim the Lucky Loser spot and a reprieve into Round 2.

### Three Stars of the Week

**1st Star: Michael Murphy (Don Cherry's, Forward)**
A playoff dynamo, Murphy tallied an impressive 5 points (3 goals, 2 assists) across two games, driving Don Cherry's dominant sweep of the Muffin Men. His consistent offensive threat ensured their swift advancement.

**2nd Star: Kosta Likourezos (The Sahara, Forward)**
Contributing 3 points (2 goals, 1 assist) this week, Likourezos proved clutch, scoring the pivotal game-tying goal in Game 2 against 4 Lines (1:03, 3rd period), a crucial marker that secured The Sahara's series victory.

**3rd Star: Adam Miller (4 Lines, Forward)**
Despite his team's elimination, Miller delivered a commendable 3 goals over two games. Notably, his third-period goal in Game 2 (10:48, 3rd period) against The Sahara kept his team in contention, demonstrating significant individual impact.
