## Top Seeds Don Cherry's, The Sahara Clinch Semi-Finals, Set Up Anticipated Final Showdown

The Low B playoffs delivered a decisive week, with both top-seeded squads sealing their spots in the championship round, extinguishing any lingering hopes of an upset.

### Flat-Earthers vs. Don Cherry's

The #1 seed Don Cherry's arrived Monday night fresh off a 3-3 tie in Game 1 of their Semi-Finals series against the Flat-Earthers, a result that hinted at a closer battle than their regular season records suggested. Any such notions were quickly dispelled in Game 2, as Don Cherry's throttled the Flat-Earthers 6-2 to clinch the series 3-1. Flat-Earthers' Ethan Hirst drew first blood on the power play, momentarily shaking the league's top team. But Don Cherry's responded within seconds with a shorthanded marker from Michael Murphy, assisted by Conor Pang, a gut punch that immediately swung momentum.

From there, it was the Cherry's show. Koch Tsatsos took over, scoring two goals, including a power-play tally, showcasing the offensive depth that has defined their season. Jack Pirie added a goal and two assists, while Nik Tsatsos and Ryan Willms rounded out the scoring, with Willms' late third-period goal driving the final nail. Flat-Earthers' Kris Jansen found the net once more in the third, but by then, the game was well out of reach. Don Cherry's rolled a full bench and dominated puck possession, reflecting their regular season form (12-2-6, +33 goal differential). The Flat-Earthers, who finished second in the regular season, simply couldn't match Don Cherry's pace or finishing ability when it mattered most. Don Cherry's now moves on to the Finals, proving their regular-season dominance was no fluke.

### The Shockers vs. The Sahara

Wednesday night saw a similar script unfold as The Sahara faced off against The Shockers, with the series tied at one point apiece following a 5-5 Game 1 draw. The Sahara, the #2 seed in the playoffs, made quick work of The Shockers in Game 2, delivering a resounding 6-1 victory to claim their Semi-Finals series 3-1. Nolan Klumak was the architect of destruction, potting a hat trick that included two goals in the first two periods, setting the tone early. Drew Ingall opened the scoring, and Derrick Wong added another, giving Sahara a commanding lead they would not relinquish.

The game also saw a curious outburst of major penalties in the second period, with Brendan Hancock and Mac Savage of The Sahara, alongside Caden Bower and Levi Tobe of The Shockers, all receiving five-minute fighting majors. While not indicative of a close game, it certainly suggested the emotional intensity of a playoff elimination match. Wil Nenadovic and Klumak's third of the night in the third period merely solidified the inevitable. The Shockers' lone goal came from Gabe Fischer-Schmidt in the first period, a mere footnote in The Sahara's dominant performance. The Sahara, a team that has found its stride in the postseason, secured their spot in the Finals, showcasing offensive firepower and an edge that the Shockers couldn't overcome.

### The Dispatch Three Stars

**1st Star: Nolan Klumak (The Sahara)** - 3G, 0A, 3Pts. - Klumak's hat trick in a clinching game was a statement. He took control, scoring the goals that broke the Shockers' spirit and propelled The Sahara into the Finals.

**2nd Star: Koch Tsatsos (Don Cherry's)** - 2G, 1A, 3Pts. - Two crucial goals and an assist from Tsatsos solidified Don Cherry's dominant Game 2 performance, ensuring the top seed advanced without unnecessary drama.

**3rd Star: Jack Pirie (Don Cherry's)** - 1G, 2A, 3Pts. - Pirie's three-point night, including a timely goal and two setups, was a key factor in Don Cherry's offensive explosion against the Flat-Earthers, showcasing his playmaking ability.
