Playoff Brackets Solidify as Contenders Advance
The "Race to Three" Concludes for Half the Division, Setting Up a Tense Lucky Loser Scenario

The opening round of the Monday/Wednesday Low B playoffs saw swift conclusions for two series, while one matchup remained a nail-biter until the very end. Games were spread across St. Mikes Arena this week, with the pressure palpable on the ice.

Don Cherry's asserted their regular-season dominance over the Muffin Men. On Wednesday, Don Cherry's secured a decisive 5-1 victory. Michael Murphy and Sean Murphy each posted a goal and an assist, with Michael Murphy's power-play marker in the second period extending their lead. Muffin Men's lone tally came from Cosimo Morin in the first. Carrying a 2-0 series lead into Monday's rematch, Don Cherry's delivered a commanding 6-0 shutout. Michael Murphy struck twice again, and Sean Murphy added two more, sealing the series sweep with a perfect 4-0 point total.

The series between 4 Lines and The Sahara delivered high-octane hockey. Their initial encounter on Wednesday was a back-and-forth 6-4 contest, with The Sahara emerging victorious. Adam Miller notched two goals for 4 Lines, including an early first-period marker, while Shane Ferguson contributed a shorthanded goal in the third. The Sahara countered with goals from six different players, including a goal and an assist from Kosta Likourezos. Holding a 2-0 advantage, The Sahara needed only a tie in Monday's game to clinch. A tight 1-1 battle ensued, with Adam Miller once again finding the back of the net for 4 Lines in the third, only to be matched by Kosta Likourezos for The Sahara. The deadlock secured The Sahara's passage to Round 2 with a 3-1 series lead.

The Flat-Earthers and The Shockers engaged in a tight affair. Their Wednesday opener finished in a 1-1 stalemate, with Marcus Simmonds scoring for the Flat-Earthers and Alex Matheson responding for The Shockers. The competitive spirit carried into Monday's decisive Game 2 at St. Mikes Arena, described as a battle where The Shockers came out on top 2-1. Flat-Earthers' Reid Chessell capitalized on a power play in the second, but The Shockers rallied with goals from Keelan Hughes and Patrick Mcmullan in the third, clinching their series 3-1.

### Series Math & The Lucky Loser

Don Cherry's (4 pts), The Sahara (3 pts), and The Shockers (3 pts) have all successfully advanced to Round 2. Among the defeated, the "Lucky Loser" spot fell to the Flat-Earthers. Despite being tied with 4 Lines at 1 point, the Flat-Earthers claimed the tiebreaker by virtue of a superior goal differential (-1 compared to 4 Lines' -2). They will now get a second life to contend for the cup.

### Three Stars of the Week (Playoff Performance)

1.  **Michael Murphy (Don Cherry's):** 3 Goals, 2 Assists (5 points) – Spearheaded Don Cherry's dominant sweep with prolific scoring and crucial playmaking.
2.  **Sean Murphy (Don Cherry's):** 3 Goals, 1 Assist (4 points) – A consistent offensive presence, his goals were integral in securing their series victory.
3.  **Dongyeon Kim (Don Cherry's):** 2 Goals, 2 Assists (4 points) – Provided reliable offensive production, a key component in Don Cherry's top-ranked performance.
