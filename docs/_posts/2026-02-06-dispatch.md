Another week, another set of pivotal matchups in the Monday/Wednesday Low B division, solidifying some positions and shaking up others. The top-ranked Don Cherry's reaffirmed their dominance, while the scramble for playoff positioning intensified amongst the middle-tier clubs.

Don Cherry's continued their impressive run with a resounding 9-2 victory over the Muffin Men at St. Mikes Arena on Monday evening. It was a clinic, with goals coming from Cassady Louks (2), Nik Tsatsos (2), and singles from Michael Murphy, Koch Tsatsos, Dongyeon Kim, Ryan Willms, and Jack Pirie. Sean Murphy dished out three assists, showcasing the team's potent offensive depth. The Muffin Men's Owen Burry and Charles Levy managed to break through, but it was largely Don Cherry's show.

In a thrilling 6-6 stalemate at St. Mikes, The Sahara and Flat-Earthers traded blows, sharing the points in a high-octane affair. The Sahara's Kosta Likourezos netted a hat-trick, while Derrick Wong, Andrew Biggs, and Mac Savage added singles. For the Flat-Earthers, Reid Chessell, Kris Jansen, Colin Lata, Zach Ladd, Marcus Simmonds, and Anthony Pennino all found the back of the net. Anthony Pennino was a playmaking force with three assists, complementing his goal. Officials Jeremy Hutchins and Shane Bowler handled a moderately active game, calling four penalties.

The Shockers snatched a crucial 4-3 win against 4 Lines in a tightly contested match at Upper Canada College School Arena, kicking off at 10:30 PM. Caden Bower proved instrumental for The Shockers with a goal and three assists, leading a comeback alongside goals from Marcus Gwyn-Neumann, Maxime Gagnon (PP), and Nick Chong (SH). 4 Lines kept it close, with Adam Miller scoring twice and Ethan Lowe adding another. Referees Russell Nichols and Eric Beltrami had a busy night, dishing out six penalties in a spirited contest.
