**Don Cherry's Cap Off Dominant Season, Claim DMHL Low B Championship Title**

The Low B crown belongs to Don Cherry's, who completed their wire-to-wire season with a decisive victory, silencing The Sahara in the grand finale and cementing their place atop the league.

The final contest, a 5-2 triumph over The Sahara on Wednesday, March 25th, at St. Mikes Arena, wasn't a cakewalk from the opening face-off. The Sahara came out swinging, with Kosta Likourezos and Wil Nenadovic finding the back of the net in the first period to stake an early lead. However, champions respond, and Don Cherry's wasted no time asserting their authority. Jack Pirie started the comeback in the second period, followed quickly by a pair of markers from Sean Murphy, one assisted by Daniel Swift and Dongyeon Kim, the other by Kim and John Gullane. These rapid-fire goals swung the momentum and the lead, putting Cherry's up 3-2 after two frames. The third period saw Ryan Willms extend the lead, and Pirie netted his second of the game to seal the 5-2 victory, locking down the championship trophy.

This season, the narrative of Don Cherry's wasn't merely about winning; it was about sheer, unyielding dominance. They sat atop the regular season standings with 30 points, boasting a league-best +33 goal differential, a testament to their consistent offensive output and defensive resolve. As the calendar turned to playoffs, the script remained unchanged. Cherry's navigated the post-season bracket with a near-perfect 4-0-1 record, leading all playoff teams with 9 points and a formidable +17 differential. Their journey was less a Cinderella story and more a coronation, proving that regular season excellence can, in fact, translate directly to playoff glory. Meanwhile, The Sahara's path to the finals, finishing fourth in the regular season with a -22 goal differential, was a commendable testament to their ability to elevate their game when it mattered most, ultimately falling just short of the ultimate prize. The Flat-Earthers, after a strong second-place regular season finish, found the playoff landscape a tougher climb, sliding to fourth in the post-season standings.

The individual brilliance displayed throughout the season cannot be overlooked. A few names consistently lit up the scoresheets, spearheading their teams' offensive attacks. The top point-getters proved to be the engines of their respective offenses.

Michael Murphy's 41 points stand as a remarkable achievement, a full seven points clear of his closest rival. His consistent offensive production was a driving force behind Don Cherry's championship run, flanked by the equally impressive Sean Murphy and the prolific Ryan Willms, who shared third place with Muffin Men's Charles Levy.

**The Final Dispatch Three Stars**:

**First Star: Sean Murphy (Don Cherry's)** - 2G, 1A, 3 Pts. - Murphy's offensive outburst in the second period, scoring two goals and assisting on another, was the turning point that put Don Cherry's in control of the championship game. His ability to produce under pressure proved critical.

**Second Star: Jack Pirie (Don Cherry's)** - 2G, 0A, 2 Pts. - Pirie's goals, including the one that kicked off Don Cherry's scoring and the one that cemented the victory in the third, showcased his knack for timely production when his team needed it most.

**Third Star: Dongyeon Kim (Don Cherry's)** - 0G, 2A, 2 Pts. - Kim's two assists on Sean Murphy's crucial second-period goals were instrumental in turning the tide, demonstrating his playmaking vision in the championship clincher.
