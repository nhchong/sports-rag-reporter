**Don Cherry's Saved by Extra Attacker as Playoff Semis Begin With Jolt**
The top seed nearly falters in Game 1; other series also knotted after opening battles.

The DMHL Low B Playoffs opened with a jarring reminder that regular season dominance means precisely nothing in the postseason. Top-seeded Don Cherry's, fresh off a commanding Round 1 sweep against Muffin Men, found themselves in unfamiliar territory, trailing the Flat-Earthers late in Game 1 and needing an extra attacker goal from Ryan Willms to force a 3-3 draw. Meanwhile, The Sahara mounted their own multi-goal comeback to tie The Shockers 5-5 in a wildly contested affair, setting a high-octane tone for the Semi-Finals. These opening skirmishes confirm one thing: this postseason is going to be a grind.

### Don Cherry's vs. Flat-Earthers: Game 1 Ends in 3-3 Stalemate

The league's top regular-season squad, Don Cherry's, almost stumbled out of the gate in Game 1. Flat-Earthers, a team that’s proven they belong in this round, came out firing, quickly building a 2-0 lead in the first period on goals from Brandon Sanders and Ethan Hirst. Don Cherry's, known for their offensive depth, struggled to break through early, but eventually found their footing. Nik Tsatsos notched two crucial goals in the third period, bringing his team back level. However, Sanders wasn't finished, netting his second of the game to restore the Flat-Earthers’ lead. It took pulling the goalie for an extra attacker in the dying seconds for Don Cherry's to salvage a point, with Willms burying the equalizer, assisted by Michael Murphy and Dongyeon Kim, with just 34 seconds left on the clock. The #1 seed avoided an early series deficit, but Flat-Earthers made a statement: they're not here to be a stepping stone. Game 2 will determine who moves on, or if goal differential becomes the decider.

### The Sahara vs. The Shockers: 5-5 Rollercoaster Leaves Both Sides Wanting More

If Don Cherry's-Flat-Earthers was a scare, The Sahara-Shockers tilt was a full-blown rollercoaster. Both teams rolled full benches, contributing to a high-scoring, back-and-forth affair. The Shockers stormed out of the gate with a three-goal outburst in the first period from Marcus Gwyn-Neumann, Nick Chong (on the power play), and Gabe Fischer-Schmidt. The Sahara managed to get one back from Andrew Biggs before the intermission. The second period saw Kosta Likourezos get The Sahara closer, but Fischer-Schmidt struck again for The Shockers. Likourezos answered back, making it a 4-3 game heading into the third. The Sahara's Wes Martin then tied it up, only for Keelan Hughes to restore The Shockers' lead on the power play. Finally, Martin found the back of the net a second time for The Sahara, knotting the game at 5-5. The Shockers watched multiple leads evaporate, but The Sahara proved their resilience, clawing back repeatedly. With the series tied 1-1 in points, Game 2 is essentially a winner-take-all scenario.

### The Dispatch Three Stars

**1st Star: Ryan Willms (Don Cherry's)** - 1G, 0A, 1 Pts. - Willms played the hero, scoring the extra attacker goal with just 34 seconds remaining to snatch a crucial tie for the top-ranked Don Cherry's, preventing a shock Game 1 loss.

**2nd Star: Caden Bower (The Shockers)** - 0G, 3A, 3 Pts. - Bower was a constant offensive threat for The Shockers, setting up three goals in their back-and-forth battle against The Sahara, showcasing his elite playmaking vision.

**3rd Star: Brandon Sanders (Flat-Earthers)** - 2G, 0A, 2 Pts. - Sanders stepped up for the underdog Flat-Earthers, scoring two pivotal goals against the league's best team and nearly securing a stunning Game 1 upset.
