**THE RUNDOWN: THIS WEEK'S HIGH (AND LOW) LIGHTS**

1.  **The Shockers Shocked The Muffin Men Early (and Often) on Monday Night.** Kicking off the week at 8:00 PM at St. Mike's, The Shockers put on an offensive clinic against the Muffin Men, rolling to a commanding 7-3 victory. Caden Bower was absolutely dialled in, potting **four goals** in the first two periods alone. The Muffin Men managed to find their offensive rhythm in the third with two goals from Charles Levy and one from Alan Wren, but by then, the game was already packed away like last year's Christmas decorations.
2.  **Don Cherry's and Flat-Earthers Play to a Gritty Draw.** Wednesday night's 8:00 PM tilt at St. Mike's was a back-and-forth affair, ending in a 5-5 tie. Don Cherry's looked to have it in the bag, holding a late lead after Sean Murphy and Koch Tsatsos scored in the third. But the Flat-Earthers, true to their stubborn nature, clawed back. Anthony Pennino scored the crucial equalizer with just **1:30 left on the clock** in the third period. A truly valiant effort, or maybe Don Cherry's just ran out of gas.
3.  **The Sahara Outlasts 4 Lines in Late-Night Marathon.** In the 10:00 PM slot on Wednesday, also at St. Mike's (the refs must have had their sleeping bags ready), The Sahara edged out 4 Lines 5-4. Kosta Likourezos was the offensive engine for The Sahara, bagging a **hat trick**, including what proved to be the game-winning goal just before the halfway mark of the third period. This one felt a little wilder than the others, with some noticeable extracurriculars.
4.  **St. Mike's: Not Quite a Penalty Trap, But 4 Lines Found the Box.** While St. Mike's Arena didn't seem to have a particular "penalty trap" vibe for most teams this week, the same couldn't be said for 4 Lines. In their close loss to The Sahara, 4 Lines racked up 6 PIMs on three separate infractions (two from Jamie Wilson alone), compared to The Sahara's single penalty. Tough to win when you're short-handed, especially when the other team is scoring late.
5.  **The "Late-Night Fade" Was More Like a "Late-Game Grit Check."** We looked for evidence of teams collapsing in the 3rd period of late games, but the data this week doesn't entirely support a universal "10:30 PM fade." While the 10:00 PM 4 Lines game saw the Sahara score the winner in the third, it was the 8:00 PM Don Cherry's vs. Flat-Earthers game that had the truly clutch, tie-making goal in the final two minutes. Seems like cardio is an equal opportunity killer, regardless of puck drop time.
