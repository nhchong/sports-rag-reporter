### The Tape Study: Discipline vs. Production

The early season often reveals which teams have a handle on their game, both offensively and defensively, and how discipline (or lack thereof) plays a role. Looking at the latest action, some patterns emerge that echo through the season's standings.

Don Cherry's, sitting atop the league, exemplifies the "play smart, stay out of the box" mantra. With a league-low 45 penalty minutes across 15 games, their discipline allows their league-leading offense (65 GF) and potent 21.7% power play to dictate play. Their tie against 4 Lines, where they converted on a power play themselves, shows they can capitalize when opponents falter.

Conversely, teams like The Shockers and Flat-Earthers have seen their penalty minutes climb. The Shockers, with 66 PIMs in 16 games, are near the top in that department, and it arguably cost them against The Sahara. That late body contact penalty created the opportunity for Sahara's go-ahead goal, proving that even recreational hockey hinges on staying out of the sin bin at critical moments. Flat-Earthers, with 61 PIMs in 14 games, also find themselves in the middle of the pack, and while they scored four goals against the Muffin Men, a cleaner sheet might have changed the outcome.

The Sahara, despite a solid 35.0% power play, also carries a higher PIM count (46 in 15 games). While their power play converted for a crucial goal against The Shockers, consistency in discipline will be key to moving up from their current fifth-place standing. It's a fine line between playing gritty and playing foolish, and the best teams manage to walk it.
